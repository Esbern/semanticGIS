# The Architecture of a Geospatial Project

The success of any geospatial endeavour lies not in the mastery of a specific software interface, but in the establishment of a **sound geospatial practice**. This practice is built upon a structured, transparent, and well-documented process.

We organise this practice around two parallel, interacting tracks:

1. **The Workflow (The Five Phases):** A chain of five well-defined stages: _Scoping, Data Modelling, Establishing the Foundation, Analytical Modelling,_ and _Dissemination_. While presented linearly, this is an iterative process; discoveries in the "Foundation" phase often require a "backflow" to refine the "Data Model."
    
2. **Project Stewardship (The Design Rationale):** A continuous layer of governance that runs alongside every phase. Stewardship ensures the work remains organised and reproducible.


 

## 1.4 The Design Rationale: The Documentation of Intent

If the Geospatial Project is the "System," the **Design Rationale (DR)** is its operating manual. In this book, we re-purpose the DR from a static "after-the-fact" report into a **Living Document**—a persistent record of the practitioner’s mental model.

### Spatial Problems as "Wicked Problems"

Following the philosophy of **Horst Rittel and Melvin Webber**, we treat geospatial projects as **"Wicked Problems."** Unlike "tame" problems (like solving an equation), wicked problems have no definitive formulation and no clear "right or wrong" answer—only "better or worse" resolutions.

Because spatial problems are wicked, they cannot be "solved" by a tool; they must be **argued** into existence.

### The Logic of Argumentation: IBIS

To manage this, the Design Rationale adopts an **Issue-Based Information System (IBIS)** approach. We do not simply record what we did; we record the "Argumentative Process":

- **Issues:** What spatial question are we answering? (e.g., "How do we define a 'Party Zone'?")
    
- **Positions:** What are the possible data sources or methods? (e.g., "Use OSM data" vs "Use CVR data").
    
- **Arguments:** Why did we choose one over the other? What were the trade-offs in terms of accuracy, cost, or ethics?
    

### Preventing Technical Drift

The primary role of the DR is to prevent **Technical Drift**—the phenomenon where an analyst changes their research question simply because a specific software button makes a different analysis easier.

By requiring every technical step to be justified in the DR, we ensure that the **Human Intent** remains the master of the **Software Spade**. This documentation is what makes a project truly **Reproducible** and **Transparent**, providing the "Semantic Grounding" necessary for both human collaborators and AI agents to understand the _logic_ behind the _map_
