# Phase 4: The Analytical Recipe — Decoupling Logic from Execution

In traditional GIS, students are often taught to "click their way" to an answer. They follow a sequence of software buttons—_Buffer, Clip, Intersect_—without ever documenting the underlying logic. In **SemanticGIS**, we reject this "tool-first" approach in favour of **Documentation Driven Development (DDD)**.

Phase 4 is about writing the **Analytical Recipe**. This is a human-readable, logically sound description of your analysis that exists independently of any software.

### 1. The Principle: Think First, Code Later

The goal of this phase is to move from the **Data Sanctuary** to a **Scientific Result** by defining a logical pipeline. We treat the analysis as a "Recipe" that can be "compiled" into different languages:

- **The Recipe (Intent):** "Identify all residential buildings within 200m of a noise-complaint cluster."
    
- **The Compilation (Extent):** This could be a Python script using `geopandas`, a SQL query in `PostGIS`, or a model in `QGIS`.
    

By writing the recipe first, you ensure that your science is driven by your **Intent**, not by the limitations of your software's user interface.

### 2. Documentation Driven Development (DDD)

In Phase 4, your **Design Rationale** becomes your development environment. You do not open your GIS software yet. Instead, you write the "Documentation" of your analysis first.

- **The Abstract Logic:** You define the spatial predicates (the "if/then" rules of your world).
    
- **The Threshold Justification:** You record _why_ you chose a 500m search radius or a specific classification method.
    

This creates a **Transparent Provenance**. If someone questions your results, you don't show them a map; you show them the Recipe.

## 3. Case Study: The Party Zone Recipe

_To illustrate Phase 4, let us look at how we define the logic for identifying Copenhagen's entertainment hotspots._

### The Analytical Recipe (from the Design Rationale):

1. **Define Intensity:** We will use **Kernel Density Estimation (KDE)** to transform our grounded bar locations into a continuous surface of "vibrancy."
    
2. **The Parameters:** We choose a **Quartic Kernel** with a **500m radius**.
    
    - _Rationale:_ 500m represents the "Relational Reach" of a venue—the distance a typical patron is willing to walk between establishments.
        
3. **Synthesis (Boolean Logic):** We will then intersect this "Vibrancy Surface" with our "Late Night" filter from Phase 3.
    
    - _Logic:_ [High Density] **AND** [Open after 02:00].
        
4. **The Result:** The final output will be a "Party Potential" raster.
    

### The AI as the Compiler

Once this recipe is written in your Design Rationale, you can hand it to an **AI Agent**. Because the agent is **Grounded** in your Phase 3 Sanctuary, you can provide a prompt like:

> _"Using the Analytical Recipe in my Rationale, write a Python script that performs a KDE on the `consecrated_bars` layer in my GeoPackage. Use a 500m radius and save the result as `party_intensity.tif`."_

The AI handles the **Extent** (the Python syntax), while you remain the master of the **Intent** (the scientific logic).
