## Phase 2: Data Modelling — Writing the Ontological Will

In the **SemanticGIS** framework, Data Modelling is not a technical task of creating database tables; it is the act of designing the **Conceptual Ontology**. We are defining the "Cast of Characters" for our project and establishing the rules of their existence.

To ensure our project becomes an **Autonomous Orphan**, we do not model for ourselves—we model for the **Adoptive Parent** (the AI Agent or the future researcher). We achieve this by defining our attributes through the **NOIR Scale of Measurement**.

### 1. The Concept: The Semantic DNA (NOIR)

When an AI agent "adopts" your project, it needs to know what mathematical operations are logically permissible. If you provide a column of numbers, the AI does not know if they are postcodes (Nominal) or population counts (Ratio). By defining the **NOIR level**, you provide the "Genetic Code" of the data:

- **Nominal:** Qualitative labels (e.g., 'Bar' vs 'Park'). Only counting and grouping are allowed.
    
- **Ordinal:** Ranked categories (e.g., 'Low', 'Medium', 'High' noise). Medians are allowed; means are not.
    
- **Interval:** Scales where the difference is meaningful, but there is no true zero (e.g., Temperature, Time of Day).
    
- **Ratio:** Quantitative values with a true zero (e.g., Distance, Capacity). All mathematical operations are allowed.

## 2. Integrated Case Study: The Copenhagen Party Zones

_In this phase, we move from the "Core Question" (Phase 1) to the "Attribute Ontology." We document this in the Design Rationale to ensure the project’s intent is inherited correctly._

### Design Rationale [2.2]: Data Modelling

> **Issue:** How do we represent 'Vibrancy' and 'Nightlife Intensity' as logical entities?
> 
> **The Conceptual Cast:** To answer the City Council's brief, we need three primary entities. We must define their **Intent** before we search for their **Extent** (the data).
> 
> 1. **The Entertainment Venue:** A point entity representing a source of social activity.
>     
> 2. **The Transit Hub:** A point entity representing the "Gateway" for patrons.
>     
> 3. **The Noise Incident:** A proxy entity for "Vibrant Friction" between residents and nightlife.
>     
> 
> **The Attribute Ontology (The Orphan's Inheritance):** I have defined the following schema for the **Adoptive Parent**. This ensures that any AI Agent assisting in the analysis understands the logical constraints of the attributes.

|Entity|Attribute|Logical Type (NOIR)|Semantic Intent|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Venue**|`Venue_Category`|**Nominal**|Distinguishes between Bars, Clubs, and Pubs.|
|**Venue**|`Vibrancy_Weight`|**Ordinal**|A 1-5 rank of 'loudness' based on the venue type.|
|**Venue**|`Opening_Time`|**Interval**|Used to filter the 'Late Night' economy.|
|**Venue**|`Capacity_Limit`|**Ratio**|The maximum number of persons allowed (Weight for KDE).|
|**Incident**|`Complaint_Type`|**Nominal**|Categorises the source (Music, Shouting, Litter).|


### 3. The Logic of the "Orphan" in Phase 2

By documenting this table in **Phase 2**, we create a "Contract of Intent."

- **Human Benefit:** If a collaborator joins the project, they don't have to guess if a `3` in `Vibrancy_Weight` is a measurement or a category.
    
- **AI Benefit:** When the AI Agent enters the **Data Sanctuary** in the next phase, it will read this ontology and automatically know that it **cannot** calculate the "average" `Venue_Category`, but it **can** calculate the "density" of `Capacity_Limit`.
    

### 4. Preparing for Grounding

This model is intentionally **decoupled** from the data. We have not yet looked at OSM or the CVR register. We have simply stated what we _need_. In **Phase 3: Grounding**, we will perform the "Sanctitisation Rituals" required to map messy, real-world datasets into this clean, logical NOIR structure.
