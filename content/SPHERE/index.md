This view organises spatial data by **semantic domain** or **sphere of interest**. Rather than thinking about "which registry?", think about "what kind of questions am I asking?"

## What is a Sphere?

Spheres are part of the **[[SPHERE]]** protocol (Semantic Protocol for Human and Earth Relational Environments). Each sphere is a thematic cluster of interests and phenomena, forming the branches of the SPHERE tree-crown model. Within each sphere, **[[Leaves/index|leaves]]** decompose datasets into purpose-specific semantic aspects that answer specific kinds of questions.

For example:

- **Socio-Technical**: Companies, people, governance, infrastructure, services, thematic perception, flow systems
- **Atmosphere**: Air, climate, weather patterns
- **Biosphere**: Ecosystems, forests, wildlife
- **Hydrosphere**: Water bodies, precipitation, water quality
- **Lithosphere**: Geology, soil, minerals, subsurface

## How Spheres Work

Each sphere describes:

1. **Semantic scope**: What phenomena it covers
2. **Subclasses**: Thematic sub-domains within the sphere
3. **Relevant collections**: Links to actual data registries (from [[Datasets by Collection]]) that provide data for this sphere

This is *semantic routing*—you navigate by interest area, then drill down to specific datasets.

## Two Navigation Paths for Data Discovery

| If you know... | Use this | Advantage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The question you want to answer (air quality, company clusters, water availability) | **[[SPHERE]]** | Semantic/thematic navigation; discover what data is available for your topic |
| The registry or dataset name | **[[Datasets by Collection]]** | Direct access to registry details, structure, and metadata |

---

## Getting Started

1. **Explore by interest**: Browse a sphere that matches your research question
2. **Find relevant collections**: Each sphere lists which data registries provide information for that domain
3. **Access the data**: Follow links to collection pages for technical access details (GraphQL queries, WFS endpoints, download options)
